AEO FAQ

Your Questions About AEO and AI Search. Answered Straight.

No fluff. No jargon. Just what you actually need to know.

What is AEO and how is it different from SEO?

SEO gets you found on Google. AEO gets you recommended by AI. When someone types a question into ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews, those engines don't show a list of links — they synthesize an answer and cite the sources they trust most. AEO is the practice of making sure your business is one of those sources. Think of SEO as getting your hand up in class. AEO is getting called on.

Why can't ChatGPT find my business?

Because AI engines don't crawl the web the same way Google does. They look for structured, trustworthy, consistently formatted content that directly answers questions. Most business websites are built to look good to humans — not to be read and understood by AI. If your site doesn't have clear signals of authority, properly structured content, and schema markup, you're effectively invisible to AI no matter how good your product or service is.

How long does it take to start showing up in AI search?

Faster than most people expect — if it's done correctly. We typically see measurable movement within 60 to 90 days for businesses that implement AEO properly from the start. The key word there is correctly. Bolting on a few FAQ sections and calling it done won't move the needle. A systematic approach across all Five Pillars of AEO is what creates lasting visibility.

Do I need to rebuild my entire website to do AEO?

Not necessarily. In most cases we work with what you have and optimize incrementally — adding schema markup, restructuring content, improving trust signals, and filling content gaps. That said, if your site is technically broken or built on a platform that limits what we can do, sometimes a rebuild is the faster path. We assess that in the first conversation, not after you've paid for months of work.

Can a small business really compete with big companies in AI search?

Yes — and this is one of the things I find most exciting about AEO right now. AI engines don't care how big your company is. They care how authoritative, structured, and trustworthy your content is. A small plumbing company in Nashville with a properly optimized site can outrank a national chain in AI recommendations for local searches. The playing field is more level than it has ever been — but only for businesses that move now, before their competitors figure this out.

Is AEO just a trend or is this actually the future of search?

One in ten US internet users now turns to generative AI first for online search, and Google AI Overviews appear in 16% of all Google desktop searches. This is not a trend. This is the permanent shift in how people find information and make buying decisions. The businesses treating it like a fad are the ones who will be invisible in two years wondering what happened.

What exactly do you do that's different from what other agencies offer?

Most agencies are still selling SEO and calling it AEO. What we do is implement AI across your entire business — not just your website. Visibility in AI search is one piece of it. But we also look at how AI can improve your lead follow-up, your content production, your operations, and your sales process. The businesses that dominate their markets over the next five years won't just be visible to AI — they'll be running on it.
